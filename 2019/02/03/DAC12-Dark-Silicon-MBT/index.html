Dark Silicon

  • Where once we would spend exponentially increasing amounts of silicon area to buy performance, now, we will spend exponentially increasing amounts of silicon area to buy energy efficiency.

Utilization Wall

The cause of dark silicon

$S=1.4x$

  • transistor count - $S^2​$
  • transistor switching frequency - $S$

computing performance - $S^3=2.8x​$

  • transistor capacitance reduce - $S​$
  • In Dennard Scaling, the voltage - $S$

Energy efficiency - $S^2$

  • In Post-Dennard Scaling (leakage limited), the voltage - const

shortfall - $S^2$ per process generation

We only have energy budget to receive 1.4x improvement - either on core count or frequency increase, or the combination of both. (or more complicated factors such as application space or cache size.)

Shrinking Chip - most pessimistic

Misconceptions

  • dark does not mean unused, just not full frequency

COST

Power Issues

THE DIM HORSEMAN

General-purpose logics

  • Near-Threshold Voltage Processors

Performance cost by dropping operating voltage can be offset by incorporating more processors. Not robust to fabrication variation.

  • Bigger Caches

Use dark silicon area for caches. Increased cache sizes can carry both performance and energy benefits for miss-intensive applications.

  • CGRA

A recurring dim alternative is the use of reconfigurable logic.

The idea is to naturally lay out the datapaths of the computation in space to avoid the multiplexing costs that are inherent to processor pipelines.

The duty cycle of CGRA elements is very low, making it a potential fit exploiting dark silicon.

Like to look at old designs with fresher eyes.

  • Computational Sprinting and Turbo Boost

To use “temporal dimness” as opposed to “spatial dimness”.

THE SPECIALIZED HORSEMAN

CoDAs (Coprocessor Dominated Architectures): Execution hops among coprocessors and general purpose cores, executing where it is most efficient. At the same time, the unused cores are power- and clock- gated to keep them from consuming precious energy.

“tower-of-babel” crisis: communication between SOFTware and HARDware. e.g. CUDA not applicable between AMD and Nvidia (Over-specialization)

  • Excessive cost of programming heterogeneous hardware.
  • being obsolete as standards are revised

Requires:

  • Insulating humans from complexity of underlying systems

    • pervasively specialized hardware

  • Overcoming Amdahl-Imposed Limits on Specialization

    • Amdahl’s Law: theoretical speedup $S_{Latency}(s)=\frac{1}{(1-p)+\frac{p}{s}}{\le}\frac{1}{1-p}$
      • p: proportion of parallelizable part (benefiting from improved resources)
      • s: speedup of parallelizable part
      • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amdahl%27s_law
      • a roadblock
    • to find broad-based specialization approaches that handles not only regular, parallel code, but also irregular code

THE DEUS EX MACHINA HORSEMAN

Deus Ex Machina a plot device in literature or theatre in which the protagonists seem utterly doomed, and then something completely unexpected and unforeshadowed comes out of nowhere to save the day.

  • Nano-Electro-Mechanical switches: physical switches
